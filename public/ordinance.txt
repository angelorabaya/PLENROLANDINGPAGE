AUTHORED: HONORABLE DEXTER B. YASAY

AN ORDINANCE REGULATING THE EXTRACTION, DISPOSITION, UTILIZATION AND PROCESSING OF QUARRY AND OTHER MINERAL RESOURCES WITHIN THE TERRITORIAL JURISDICTION OF THE PROVINCE OF MISAMIS ORIENTAL AND AMENDING VARIOUS TAXES AND FEES THEREFORE.
       WHEREAS, the Sangguniang Panlalawigan finds that it is high time to amend the 20l 7 Revenue Code of the Province of Misamis Oriental concerning the tax on Sand, Gravel and other Quarry Resources to improve the locally sourced income and fortify efficient revenue collection;
       WHEREAS, all the necessary requirements prescribed under the Local Government Code for tax ordinances and revenue measures have been properly complied with;
       WHEREAS, Section 19 1 of the Local Government Code of 1991 explicitly provides that Local Government Units shall have the authority to adjust the tax rates as prescribed not oftener than once every five (5) years, but in no case shall such adjustment exceed ten percent (10%) of the rates fixed under the Code;
       WHEREAS, Section 468 (a) (2) (ii) of the Code empowers the Sangguniang Panlalawigan to enact ordinances levying taxes, fees and charges, prescribing the rates thereof for general and specific purposes, and granting tax exemptions, incentives or reliefs;
       WHEREAS, the Committee on Ways and Means has recommended to enact an Ordinance Regulating the Extraction, Disposition, Utilization and Processing of Quarry and Other Mineral Resources within the territorial jurisdiction of the Province of Misamis Oriental and Amending Various Taxes and Fees therefore;

19TH SANGGUNIANG PANLALAWIGAN OF MISAMIS ORIENTAL

       WHEREAS, the Sangguniang Panlalawigan of Misamis Oriental concurs with the recommendation of the Committee;
       NOW THEREFORE, after due deliberation and on motion of Provincial Board Member Dexter B. Yasay, and duly seconded, it was;
       RESOLVED to enact, as the Sangguniang Panlalawigan hereby enacts, the following Ordinance to wit:
       
ORDINANCE NO. 1571-2022
AN ORDINANCE REGULATING THE EXTRACTION, DISPOSITION, UTILIZATION AND PROCESSING OF QUARRY AND OTHER MINERAL RESOURCES WITHIN THE TERRITORIAL JURISDICTION OF THE PROVINCE Of MISAMIS ORIENTAL AND AMENDING VARIOUS TAXES AND FEES THEREFORE.
       BE IT ORDAINED, by the Sangguniang Panlalawigan of the Province of Misamis Oriental in session assembled, that;
       SECTION 1. TITLE. This Ordinance which amends the 2017 Revenue Code of the Province of Misamis Oriental shall be known as “An Ordinance Regulating the Extraction, Disposition, Utilization and Processing of Quarry and Other Mineral Resources within the Territorial Jurisdiction of the Province of Misamis Oriental and Amending Various Taxes and Fees Therefore."
       SECTION 2. SCOPE AND APPLICATION. This Ordinance shall govern the levy, ossessmen1, taxes, fees, charges. and other impositions on the extraction, disposition, utilization and processing of quarry and other mineral resources within the territorial jurisdiction of the Province of Misamis Oriental.
       SECTION 3. AMENDMENT. SP Ordinance No. 1404-2017 entitled "2017 Revenue Code of the Province of Misamis Oriental" is hereby amended to read as follows:

Chapter 2 - Taxes
Article D. Tax on Sand, Gravel and other Quarry Resources

Section 16. Imposition of Tax. There shall be imposed a tax of ten percent (10%) of fair market value per cubic meter of ordinary stones, sand, gravel, earth and other quarry resources, as defined under the Notional Internal Revenue Code, extracted from private and public lands or from beds of seas. lakes, rivers, streams, creeks, and other private or public waters within territorial jurisdiction of the province.
For purposes of establishing the fair market value per cubic meter of sand, gravel, and other quarry resources on an annual basis. an evaluation committee is hereby created to be composed of the following:

a. Provincial Treasurer					- Chairman
b. Provincial Engineer						- Member
c. Provincial Planning and Development Officer		- Member
d. Provincial Environment and Natural Resources Officer	- Member
e. Provincial Assessor						- Member

Section 17. Permit. The permit to extract sand, gravel and other quarry resources either from private or public lands shall be issued exclusively by the Provincial Governor.
Section 17-A. Gratuitous Permit. A gratuitous permit shall be issued by the Governor to any government agency upon application and approval of the permit to extract the above-mentioned materials for the purpose of government projects within the province and shall be exempt from the payment of the taxes as prescribed.
Section 18. Proceeds. The proceeds of the tax under this Article shall be distributed as follows:
(a) Misamis Oriental - Thirty Percent (30%);
(b) Component City or Municipality where the sand, grovel and other quarry resources are extracted - Thirty Percent (30%); and,
(c) Barangay where the sand, gravel and other quarry resources are extracted - Forty Percent (40%).

Section 63. Civil Remedies for Collection of Taxes, Fees and Charges. The provision of Book II, Title I, Chapter 4, specifically from Section 172 to 185 of RA 7160 on the civil remedies for the collec1ion of delinquent taxes, fees and charges may be availed of by the Provincial Treasurer if the needs arise.
Section 63-A. Provincial Mining Regulatory Board. The PMRB shall, among others, accept, process and evaluate applications for Quarry, Sand and Gravel, Guano, Gemstone Gathering, and Small-Scale Mining Permits duly filed with the same.
a. Composition
i. The Reginal Director of the MGB or his authorized representative, as Chairman;
ii. The Provincial Governor or his authorized representative, as Vice-Chairman:
iii. Large-Scale Mining Representative;
iv. Small-Scale Mining Representative;
v. Representative from Non-governmental Organization / Environmental Sector
b. Provincial Mining Regulatory Board Secretariat. - The Provincial Local Environment and Natural Resources Officer (PLENRO) shall serve as the secretariat of the board. The secretarial shall accept and process quarry, small-scale mining permit applications, and assist the members during PMRB deliberations.
c. Honoraria of the Members of the Provincial Mining regulatory Board. The members and the secretariat of the PMRB shall receive honorarium in the amount of Two Thousand Pesos (Php 2,000.00) each, for attendance in one meeting. The support staff, referred as the technical Working Group (TWG), shall receive honorarium in the amount of One Thousand Pesos (Php 1,000.00) each, for attendance in one meeting.
The honoraria shall be disbursed from the Administrative Fees paid by the quarry or small-scale mining permit applicant/permit holder.

Section	64. Deputies of the Provincial Governor. The PLENRO, the Provincial/City/Municipal Treasurer, the Provincial Legal Officer, the City/Municipal Mayors, the elements of the Philippine National Police, and the Punong Barangays shall be the deputies of the Provincial Governor to monitor mining and quarry activities, implement the provisions and regulations stipulated in this Provincial Ordinance, and apprehend illegal mining and quarry activities within their respective territorial jurisdiction.
Section65. Authority to Apprehend. The personnel assigned to enforce the laws, rules and regulations regarding small scale mining. sand and gravel extraction and other quarry operation, is hereby authorized to apprehend illegal activities anytime and anywhere within the province.
Section 66. Requirement for Other Agencies. Agencies implementing infrastructure projects shall be required by this Code to require contractors to show certification as to proof of legal source of sand, gravel and other quarry resources to be used in the projects, during bidding process. Proof of the payment of tax (extraction fees or tax on sand and gravel) must also be required prior to payment of the billings of the contractor.
Section 67. Special Permit to Extract and Dispose. The granting of Special Permit to Extract and Dispose shall be issued to any individual who wish to develop his idle land into productive use, wherein, during the course of development, there is a need to extract and dispose specific volume of ordinary earth, limestone, sand and gravel. and other quarry materials therefrom. The permit is only valid for Six (6) Months from date of issuance and not renewable.
(a) Earth Moving Permit with Commercial Disposition. This applies to areas subjected t Scrapping. Construction, and Land Development Projects that requires the disposal of the generated excess waste materials. Below are the following requirements:
i. Letter of Intent addressed to the Provincial Governor;
ii. Sketch Plan with Vicinity Map;
iii. Title and Tax Declaration of the Site;
iv. Barangay Certification;
v. Municipal Certification;
vi. List of Equipment to be used;
vii. Volume to be extracted; and
viii. Environmental Compliance Certificate or Certificate of Non-Coverage from EMB
(b) Desilting Permit with Commercial Disposition. This applies to areas with heavily silted alluvial piles and other related mineral residues within river channels in the province. Provided, that this permit shall not supersede neither equivalent nor inconsistent to the Dredging Permit issued by the DPWH. Below are the following requirements:
i. Letter of Intent/ Justification addressed to the Provincial Governor;
ii. Survey Plan with limit to up to two (2) hec1ares;
iii. Barangay Certification;
iv. Municipal Certification;
v. List of Equipment to be used;
vi. Volume to be extracted; and
vii. Environmental Compliance Certificate or Certificate of Non-Coverage from EMB
	
Section 67-A. Authority to Transport and Dispose. In the case of expiration of permit an authority to dispose maybe issued to Permit Holder, Concessionaire and Quarry Operator for the transportation and/or disposal of any remaining quantity of sand and gravel, and other quarry resources, which have been extracted prior to such expiration, but not transported and/or disposed of. Provided, further, that the quantity to be transported and/or disposed shall not exceed that stipulated in the ECC. The period for disposal of the quarry resource covered by the authority to dispose is thirty (30) days from issuance of the same.
Section 67-B. Hauling Stickers, Maximum Allowable load Capacity per Type of Truck and Regulatory Fee.
a. Upon the effectivity of this Ordinance. all registered trucks or motor vehicles shall be marked with official hauling stickers issued by the Province. Further, only vehicles with the official hauling stickers shall be allowed to haul or transport sand, gravel and other quarry materials within the territorial jurisdiction of the Province. Any vehicle caught hauling and/or transporting sand, grovel and other quarry materials within the territorial jurisdiction without the official hauling stickers pasted or displayed in said vehicle shall be impounded and the loaded quarry materials confiscated in favor of the Province.

Section 68. Prepaid Delivery Receipt. Implementation of prepaid delivery receipt system to obtain advance payment of the sand, grovel and other quarry resources to be extracted is hereby adopted.
Section 68-A. Power to Levy Other Taxes, Fees, or Charges. The province may exercise the power to levy taxes, fees or charges on any base or subject not otherwise specifically enumerated herein or taxed under the provisions of the National Internal Revenue Code, as amended, or other applicable lows; Provide, that the taxes, fees or charges shall not be unjust, excessive, oppressive, confiscatory or contradictory to declared national policy. Provided, further, that the ordinance levying such taxes, fees or charges shall not be enacted without any prior public hearing conducted for the purpose.
Section 68-B. Authority to Adjust Rates. The Sangguniang Panlalawigan shall have the sole authority to adjust tax rates as prescribed herein not oftener that once every five (5) years, provided that such adjustment will be fair, non-discriminatory and economically justifiable.
SECTION 4. POSTING AND PUBLICATION. The Provincial Board Secretary shall cause the proper posting and publication of this Ordinance in accordance to Section 511 of the Local Government Code of 1991.
SECTION 5. EFFECTIVITY CLAUSE. This Ordinance shall take effect on the day immediately following the day of last publication.
